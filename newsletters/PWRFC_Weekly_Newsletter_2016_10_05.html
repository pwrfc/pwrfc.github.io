Captain's Log: Weekend Recap

This past weekend’s game against Raleigh was a tale of two halves.  Philly got off to a slow start and were punished for it, allowing 5 tries by Raleigh’s speedy and evasive back line in the first 30 minutes.  Although ball possession was 50/50, turn overs on offense gave Raleigh the opportunity to strike quickly as our defense re-formed.  It wasn’t until the 35th minute when Philly got our groove back.  Philly’s back line’s relentless kicking assault on Raleigh’s back three gave us just the real estate we needed to advance up the field.  With the hard charging runs of our forwards and dominating momentum of our scrums, Raleigh was on their back foot and the frustration started to show.  Our first try of the game came courtesy of Katie “Midge” Cardone, a St. Joe’s grad and scrumhalf, who took a quick tap and weaved through Raleigh’s defense for the score.  

Former Yale track stand out and social justice advocate Katie Chockley left Raleigh in the dust, scoring two more tries for Philly.  Her well-timed runs, cheetah-like speed, and composure on defense earner her the honor of Bitch of the Pitch.  Our forwards, who were at a noticeable size disadvantage, were the unsung heroes of the day.  Playing three times her size, channeling the brute strength of a grizzly bear and the intestinal fortitude of a honey badger, Rachel Miller earned the Silent Force award.  Her dedication to every ruck and tackle was unparalleled.  

At practice this week we will continue to work on our team play and build our confidence going into the next two games.  This Saturday we face Pittsburgh away.  If we come out with the heart and determination we showed in that second half, we will come out on top. 

Kate Hallinan
Captain
 

This coming weekend’s game against Pittsburgh kicks off at 1pm at the Norwin Soccer Club Turner Valley Complex in Pittsburgh.  This is a league match. We would love to have the support of our friends and family in person if they are in the area or give us a shoutout on social media!

News and Announcements

New Coaching Staff

Philly Women would like to welcome Dr. Arturo Bravo (R2) who is joining the team as the new Head Coach. R2 has played and coached rugby all over the world but has returned to the city of Philadelphia and is excited to share his knowledge with our program. His most recent coaching adventures include Head Coach of the the Northeast Philadelphia Irish, and Head Coach of Boston Women, where he carried the team to finish 3rd in the 2016 DI Northeast Division. We are excited for this new opportunity but we would like to take the time to thank Coach Bob Weir for his constant support and help in transitioning this fall. Bob has been with Philly Women for a number of years, and has kept the team at a high level of competition in Women's Division I. We truly appreciate his loyalty towards our team.
